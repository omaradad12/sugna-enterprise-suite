Data protection

Sugna Enterprise Suite serves non-governmental organizations (NGOs), humanitarian actors, and public-interest institutions that often operate under strict donor rules, duty-of-care obligations, and data minimization expectations. This policy explains how we handle personal information in connection with our public website, marketing activities, and—where applicable—contracted cloud services. Processing of data inside your production tenant is also governed by your agreement with Sugna (including any data processing or confidentiality terms) and your own policies toward beneficiaries and staff.

1. Scope of policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Sugna (“Sugna,” “we,” “us”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you visit our public website, submit forms, receive communications from us, or otherwise interact with us in a capacity covered here. It should be read together with our Terms of Use and any product-specific notices we provide at the point of collection.

If you use Sugna’s hosted platform as an authorized user of a customer organization, additional terms—including your organization’s instructions and our contractual commitments—may define how certain data is processed. Where this policy and your agreement conflict regarding production services, the agreement prevails.

2. Information collected

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect:

  • Information you provide — such as name, professional title, organization name, email address, phone number, country or region, and the content of messages you send through contact or demo request forms, event registrations, or support inquiries.
  • Technical and usage data — such as IP address, browser type and language, device type, approximate location derived from IP, referring URLs, pages viewed, and timestamps, collected through server logs and similar technologies.
  • Cookies and similar technologies — as described in the Cookies section.

We ask that you do not send us special categories of personal data (such as health information) through general website forms unless we explicitly request it for a defined purpose and provide appropriate safeguards.

3. How information is used

We use personal information for purposes such as:

  • responding to inquiries and providing customer or pre-sales support;
  • scheduling demos, webinars, or training and sending related communications;
  • operating, securing, and improving our website and underlying infrastructure;
  • detecting, preventing, and investigating fraud, abuse, or security incidents;
  • complying with legal obligations and enforcing our terms;
  • sending marketing communications where permitted by law and, where required, with your consent or an unsubscribe option.

Where European or UK data protection law applies, we rely on appropriate legal bases (such as contract, legitimate interests, consent, or legal obligation) depending on the context.

4. Data sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information with:

  • Service providers who host our site, deliver email, analyze aggregated usage, or provide security and IT services, subject to confidentiality and processing terms;
  • Professional advisers where required (for example, auditors or lawyers) under a duty of confidentiality;
  • Authorities when we believe disclosure is required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, or to protect the rights, safety, and integrity of Sugna, our users, or the public.

If Sugna is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will require the successor to honor this policy or provide notice of changes.

5. Data storage and security

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, loss, or alteration, appropriate to the sensitivity of the data and the nature of our services. These measures may include access controls, encryption in transit where applicable, monitoring, and vendor due diligence.

No method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to use commercially reasonable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Data retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law (for example, tax, accounting, or dispute resolution). Criteria for retention include the nature of the data, the potential risk of harm from unauthorized use, and whether we can achieve the purpose by other means.

When retention is no longer required, we delete or anonymize information in accordance with our internal schedules and technical capabilities.

7. User rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal information; to object to certain processing; to data portability; and to withdraw consent where processing was based on consent. You may also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

To exercise these rights, contact us using the details in Contact information. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling certain requests. If you are an end user of a Sugna customer, we may refer your request to that organization where we process data solely on its instructions.

8. Cookies

We and our service providers may use cookies, pixels, and similar technologies to remember preferences, understand how visitors use our site, and measure campaign effectiveness. You can control cookies through your browser settings; disabling cookies may affect site functionality.

Where required, we will obtain consent before placing non-essential cookies and provide a clear way to withdraw consent.

9. International transfers

Sugna may process and store information in countries other than your own. When we transfer personal data from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland to countries not deemed to provide an adequate level of protection, we implement appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses approved by relevant authorities or other lawful transfer mechanisms, supplemented by technical and organizational measures where appropriate.

10. Children’s privacy

Our website and enterprise services are not directed at children under 16 (or the age required by local law). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us and we will take steps to delete it promptly.

11. Changes to policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page reflects the effective date of the current version. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Where changes are material, we may provide additional notice as appropriate (for example, a notice on our website or an email where we have your contact details and consent).

12. Contact information

For questions about this Privacy Policy, to exercise privacy rights, or to report a concern, please use our contact page and include “Privacy Policy” in your message. For data protection inquiries related to an active subscription or production environment, please also reference your organization name and, where available, your tenant or contract identifier so we can route your request appropriately.
