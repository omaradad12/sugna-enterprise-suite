Frequently asked questions

Quick answers for IT, finance, program, and operations teams evaluating Sugna Enterprise Suite.

Is Sugna cloud-based and multi-tenant?

Yes. Sugna Enterprise Suite is delivered as a cloud service with logical tenant isolation so each organization’s data and configuration stay separated while benefiting from a unified platform lifecycle and operations.

Can we start with only one module?

Yes. You can adopt modules in phases—many teams begin with Financial & Grant Management or another priority area, then add HR, procurement & logistics, fleet, AI Auditor, hospital, or corporate capabilities when you are ready.

How does Sugna support donor compliance?

The platform supports grant structures, restrictions, budgets, utilization, and funder-ready reporting—with audit trails and approvals aligned to donor rules, internal policy, and program accountability.

Does Sugna support role-based access control?

Yes. Role-based access, segregation of duties, and configurable workflows help ensure users only see and act on data and transactions appropriate to their responsibilities.

Can Sugna manage multiple projects and entities?

Yes. Multi-entity operations, program and project dimensions, and consolidated visibility are supported so finance, programs, and leadership can align on one model without manual reconciliation across spreadsheets.

Does Sugna support audit requirements?

Yes. Journals, approvals, traceability, and AI-assisted monitoring support internal and external audit expectations across finance and operations—so evidence is available in the flow of work.

Is Sugna suitable for NGOs and hospitals?

Yes. Sugna is built for mission-driven and regulated organizations, including NGOs and healthcare providers—with capabilities for grants, donor accountability, and, where needed, clinical and financial alignment on one stack.

Can Sugna integrate with other systems?

Yes. API-ready integration patterns and enterprise deployment options support connecting to payroll, banking, identity, and other systems consistent with your architecture and governance standards.

How secure is Sugna Enterprise Suite?

Security is foundational: tenant isolation, encryption in transit and at rest, role-based access, and operational controls designed for enterprise deployments and security reviews.
